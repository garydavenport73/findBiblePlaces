After tons and tons of searching, I found something else that I think many will find VERY useful, and seeing as this is one of the top results for "google maps url parameters" I thought i'd add it here.
You can change the name of the location that is displayed in the marker window. By default it is "Address:" or I suppose something else if you put a lat/long into the q= parameter. BUT, you in the q= string, by adding parenthesis at the end.
http://maps.google.com/maps?110+South+Street+Chicago+IL+(My House)
The above will change the title of the location in the info window to whatever you put in the parenthesis.

I just discovered this post today, and it answered a lot of questions I had. ExTexan, here are some examples that helped me get rid of the extra marker (the green arrow is your search term, and the "A" marker is the nearest named thing).

Adding "loc:" to the query tells google maps that it is coordinates and not to look for things near it:
https://maps.google.com/maps?q=loc:13.795494,+100.566345&t=h&z=18&output=embedYou can also get fancy and change the text that says "Address" in the info window (plus signs are treated as spaces):
https://maps.google.com/maps?q=loc:13.795494,+100.566345+(This+is+a+house)&t=h&z=18&output=embedYou can get even fancier, and have a text query along with your title and coordinates:
https://maps.google.com/maps?q=A+house+right+over+here+(Hello+from)@13.795494,+100.566345&t=h&z=18&output=embed

(edited for formatting)
